The wedge

One capture-and-structure core, three go-to-market fronts.

Why three markets, not one

The hard part of every to-do product is the same: getting a messy human intention out of someone's head and into a shared, structured, delegable plan. We built that once. ADHD focus, co-parenting and caregiving are simply three audiences where that problem is acute enough that people search for a fix and pay for it. The same voice-to-structure engine and shared-space backbone powers all three; only the surface — the audience pack — changes.

Built-in network pull

Every space is a small network: a household, a co-parenting pair, a care team. You cannot coordinate alone, so each paying user brings in the people they coordinate with — the other parent, the sibling sharing a parent's care, the rest of the family. Growth is a property of the product's shape, not a line item in a paid-acquisition budget.

Distribution without a gatekeeper

It ships as an installable web app. No app-store review, no 15–30% platform cut, no install friction, and updates land the moment we push them. That keeps gross margin high and shortens the loop between a user's problem and our fix.

Defensibility that compounds

Append-only shared history is the backbone of trust between co-parents and carers — a record that can't be quietly rewritten. It's also switching cost: the longer a family's coordination lives here, the more it holds. Three audiences feeding one engine means every improvement to capture, structuring or sync lifts all three at once.
