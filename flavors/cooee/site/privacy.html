Privacy policy

Effective 21 July 2026 · applies to the Cooee app and website

The short version. The record belongs to the participant. Circle data is hosted in Australia. Who sees what inside a circle is enforced by server-side rules — and nothing in a circle is ever hidden from its owner. No GPS, no clock-in surveillance, no selling data, no ads, and export is free forever. You can delete your account yourself, in the app.

Who we are

Cooee is operated by its developer ("we", "the operator") in Australia. Contact options are on the support page.

What stays on your device

By default, everything you haven't shared into a circle: your own tasks, captured notes and transcripts, your private journal, photos, and settings — stored in your browser's local storage. Deleting the app's data (Settings → Your data → Delete all data) removes it from the device.

What leaves your device, and when

  • Circles and shared spaces. When you create or join a circle, that circle's content — tasks, shared notes, the append-only history, support sessions, incident reports, plan goals, expenses, receipt photos, and member display names — syncs through our hosted database (Google Firebase / Cloud Firestore, project cooee-dbde6, hosted in Sydney, Australia — australia-southeast1). Only signed-in members of the circle can read it, and only within their role's visibility (below). The one data flow that leaves Australia is Smart AI processing, described below.
  • Your account. Sign-in is anonymous by default — an ID with no name or email. If you choose Google or email sign-in, we store that email, linked to the same account. We also keep your list of memberships, your onboarding selection, and a daily Smart AI usage counter (a number only — never content).
  • Smart AI (optional). When Smart AI structures a capture, summarises a Plan Review Pack, or reads text from a photo, the relevant text or image is sent to Google's Gemini API through our Sydney-hosted proxy (which checks your daily quota and forwards the request without storing your content). Google processes it to return the result under Google's API terms; the model API call is the one step that may exit Australia. Never enable Smart AI and the built-in engine does the work entirely on your device.
  • Voice (optional). Live transcription uses your browser's speech engine (which on most browsers sends audio to the browser vendor's servers); the opt-in on-device model transcribes entirely on your device and works offline.
  • Analytics (off by default). If ever enabled, it is opt-in, anonymous and feature-level only — never what anyone typed.

Who sees what inside a circle

Visibility is enforced by server-side security rules, not just the interface, and is covered by automated tests. The honest summary every member deserves:

  • The owner (the participant or their nominee) can read everything in their circle. That is the product's founding invariant — it is the participant's record. Workers and family: nothing you write into a circle, including "private"-scoped notes, is hidden from the circle's owner or co-admins.
  • Incident reports are visible to their author and the circle's owner/co-admins only — not to other workers or viewers. Filing one notifies nobody outside the circle.
  • Viewers are read-only. Workers write within their scope. Roles can only be raised by an owner or co-admin, and every change lands in the append-only history.
  • Deliberately not collected: no GPS or location data, no clock-in/clock-out surveillance, no per-worker duration analytics. Support sessions record what happened, not where you stood or how many minutes you're worth.

What we never do

  • No selling or sharing of personal information with third parties for their purposes.
  • No advertising, no trackers. No reading your content — the operator's dashboard sees counts, not words.
  • No paywall on the record: the history, attribution, and every export (CSV, print/PDF, full backup) are free, forever. Export and leave any time.

The record persists — that's its job

A circle's history is append-only. What you contribute to a circle stays part of that circle's record for its members, attributed to your display name, even if you later leave or delete your account — the way sent messages stay with recipients. A circle that ends can be archived, or exported and removed entirely by its members.

Retention and deletion

  • Device data: delete it in Settings → Your data.
  • Account: delete it yourself in Settings → Account → Delete account. This removes your sign-in, your per-account records, and your access to every circle. Server backups age out within about 35 days.
  • Health-adjacent content (medication routines, care notes) lives only inside the circles you put it in, under the rules above — we apply the same minimal-footprint handling to all of it.

Children

Circles often support children and young people; their information is entered and managed by the adults in their circle. Accounts themselves are for people 13 and over (or the age of digital consent where you live).

Your rights

We handle personal information in line with the Australian Privacy Principles. You can access your data (export, in-app), correct it (in-app), and delete it (in-app). For anything else — including complaints — use the support page; if you're unsatisfied with our response you can contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (oaic.gov.au).

Changes

Material changes move the effective date above and are called out in release notes. We won't quietly weaken this policy — the trust promises are product constraints, not marketing.
