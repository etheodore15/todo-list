What Cooee is

Most NDIS software is bought by providers and points at the workforce: rostering, clock-ins, compliance for the employer. Cooee flips the ownership. It's the participant's coordination record — family, independent support workers, and allied health all writing into one append-only history that the participant (or their nominee) owns, can export, and can walk away with.

1 · Speak it onceA worker, parent or the participant talks (or types, or snaps a photo) — tasks, observations and expenses structure themselves. No forms at the moment of care.
2 · The circle stays in stepEverything lands in the circle with attribution: who did what, when, in which support session. New workers read the handoff instead of asking the parent to repeat it.
3 · It reads back when it mattersThe record composes into a goal-grouped Plan Review Pack, a claim-ready expense CSV, and everyday handoffs — instead of living in one exhausted person's head.

Roles & rules-enforced visibility

  • Four roles: owner (the participant or nominee), co-admin (usually family), worker, and viewer — enforced by the database's security rules, not just by the interface. A tampered client still can't read or write beyond its role.
  • Per-item visibility — circle / team / assigned / private — with one invariant that never bends: nothing is ever hidden from the owner. It's the participant's record.
  • Viewers are truly read-only: a grandparent or case manager sees everything shared with them and is never offered a writable-looking control (and keeps a private journal for their own words).
  • Invites can only mint worker or viewer. Elevation to co-admin is a deliberate act by an owner or co-admin, never something a code can grant.
  • The whole permission matrix is proven by an 80-check automated test that gates every release.

A support worker's day

  • Session brackets: tap "Start support", do the shift, tap "End support" — every tick and note in between reads as part of that session in the history and handoff. Sessions auto-close after 12 hours with an honest marker.
  • Deliberately not time-tracking: no per-worker duration totals, no GPS, no clock-in surveillance. Cooee records care, not the workforce.
  • Observations aren't tasks: "she seemed tired after the pool today" stays a note in the record — the AI and the local engine both speak circle vocabulary (participant, session talk, observations) instead of turning everything into a to-do.
  • Incident reports: structured what / when / who / action-taken, voice-fillable from the capture box, visible to the author and the circle's admins only, append-only — with plain copy that it notifies nobody outside the space.
  • Meds & routines from the shared engine: one task per dose, ticks are records, misses are flagged, and a PRN line shows the last dose and when the prescribed gap ends. The app computes, never advises.

Plan goals & the Plan Review Pack

  • Goals in the participant's words: the circle records what the supports work toward. Each goal derives a tag that the AI and the tag picker offer, so everyday work quietly accumulates under the goal it serves.
  • The Plan Review Pack: one tap composes 12 weeks of the record — goal progress, support sessions, incidents, adherence, open tasks and expenses — into a document ready for a plan review. AI-summarised with a local fallback; every render carries "Participant-owned coordination record. Not medical, legal, or NDIS advice."
  • For families who walk into plan reviews with a shoebox of memories, this is the product's defining moment: the year's evidence, already organised.

Money that's ready for a claim

  • Spoken amounts become expenses at capture ("$42 for the taxi to hydro").
  • Circle expenses carry provider, ABN and support date; receipt photos compress on-device and ride along.
  • The ledger CSV exports with support_date / provider_abn / receipt_ref columns — shaped for plan-managed and self-managed claims paperwork.

Everything the shared engine brings

Cooee is a flavor of the same engine that powers Idea → Todo — one codebase, so every coordination feature arrives in both products.

Capture

Voice, photo, text

  • Live in-browser transcription; opt-in fully offline speech-to-text
  • Photo capture: snap a letter or label — OCR into the pipeline, or the photo rides the note
  • Care-register speech extracts without action verbs ("Dad donepezil every day at 8am")
  • Honest counting: if a long monologue exceeds one capture, the app says what didn't fit
Trust layer

The record

  • Append-only history — deletes leave tombstones, nothing is silently rewritten
  • Attribution on every action
  • CSV + print/PDF export, always free
  • Spaces archive with dignity; "Export & remove" for placement changes
Daily life

Tasks that fit real weeks

  • Real-life recurrence: fortnightly, quarterly, "every three hours"
  • Delegation by voice; assignees; per-person filters
  • No-shame carry-over — nothing ever goes red
  • Daily digest and a "while you were away" banner
Read-back

Reports

  • Doctor briefing with numeric readings trends (BGL, sats, feeds…)
  • Shift handoff for the next worker
  • Plan Review Pack (Cooee's own)
  • All compose locally in an instant; AI optionally enriches; all print

Platform: an installable, offline-capable web app (no app store required), a managed zero-config backend with rules-enforced membership, on-device AI options for the privacy-sensitive, and accessibility built in.

Onboarding — built for circles that grow

  • First run: guided setup creates the circle, names the participant's space, and coaches the first capture.
  • Joining by invite: every arrival gets a 30-second welcome shaped by their role — a worker learns session brackets, plan-goal notes and private incidents; a viewer learns they can read everything and write nothing. Then straight into a first capture.
  • Professionally hand-overable: a support coordinator or OT can set the circle up with a family and let the app carry the welcome from there. (A fuller professional mode is a planned bet.)

The trust promises

No GPS. No clock-in surveillance. Australian-hosted. Export and leave any time.
Participant-owned coordination record. Not medical, legal, or NDIS advice.

These aren't marketing lines — they're product constraints. The record, attribution and exports are free forever; the permission matrix is enforced server-side and proven by tests; and there is deliberately no worker-hours reporting, because the day Cooee becomes a surveillance tool is the day workers stop writing honest notes in it.

Validation — the 44-persona study

Cooee's flows were driven end-to-end by executable persona simulations as part of a 44-persona study across both products — including NDIS circles with independent workers, young carers, palliative rosters and foster placements. Every finding ranked P0–P2 has been shipped and re-verified against the personas that surfaced it. The pilot circle is the next validation layer: real families, real workers, real plan reviews.

The business model, end to end

Who pays, and why they would

The buyer is the participant or their family — the people who own the record and walk into the plan review. Coordination costs in this cohort are real and budgeted; families routinely fund low-cost tools that reduce coordination load, often from Core–Consumables in a plan-managed or self-managed budget. (Whether a given purchase fits a given plan is between the participant and their plan manager — Cooee makes no funding claims.)

Distribution — the worker is the wedge

Workers spread itIndependent support workers serve several participants. A worker who lives in one Cooee circle introduces it to the next family — free forever means zero friction.
Coordinators hand it overSupport coordinators and OTs teach coordination habits already; role-shaped joiner onboarding makes Cooee something they can set up with a family in one visit.
The Pack closesThe first plan review with an organised, goal-grouped record is the retention event — and the neighbouring family hears about it.

Unit economics

Cooee shares Idea → Todo's engine and infrastructure — one codebase, one backend, marginal cost per circle measured in cents. At $190/yr per circle with workers free, a small number of subscribed circles sustains the product; the engine's development cost amortises across both products.

Where it is honestly at

Cooee is in pilot with a small circle. The subscription is designed and priced but not yet switched on — the pilot runs on the full product while the payment plumbing (web billing first) and the production backend cutover land. Extraction is English-only today (a multilingual plan exists, unscheduled). The full site is on its way; this page and the app are the current front door.
