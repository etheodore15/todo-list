One engine, three moves

Idea → Todo is an installable web app that turns spoken, messy intentions into structured, shared, delegated to-do lists — and turns everyday actions into a record a family can trust.

1 · CaptureTalk (live transcript), type, or snap a photo. No organising required at the moment of thought — that's the whole point.
2 · StructureAI + a hardened local engine split the ramble into tasks with dates, times, recurrence, people, energy levels and dollar amounts. Anything that isn't a task is kept as a note, never lost.
3 · Share & trustTasks land in shared spaces with attribution on every action. The history is append-only — nothing can be silently rewritten — and it reads back as per-audience reports.

Capture

  • Voice-first: near-real-time live transcription in the browser; opt-in fully offline speech-to-text (Whisper on-device) for privacy-sensitive users.
  • Understands how carers and parents actually speak: "Dad donepezil every day at 8am", "Ruby speech therapy Tuesday 10am", "Sofia needs new footy boots" — med and appointment noun-phrases extract without an action verb.
  • Photo capture: snap a school notice, appointment letter or medication label — "Read text from photo" drops the words into the capture box so the whole pipeline applies; or the photo simply rides the note into the journal and shared record.
  • Spoken money is an expense: "$38 school photo money", "I paid $180 for the carpet cleaner" carry amount, payer and date straight into the shared ledger.
  • Destination chips: every capture says where it lands — Private or any space. Read-only roles are never offered a writable-looking destination.
  • A private journal: "Save to my Journal" keeps a note (text and/or photo) on-device with no task extraction and no space record — shareable later, deliberately.
  • Honest counting: if a long monologue exceeds what one capture keeps, the app says what didn't fit. Silent loss is the one sin a capture tool can't afford.
  • AI refresh: a capture structured by the built-in engine while AI was unavailable can be re-processed later with one tap — the AI result replaces the unfinished first-pass tasks (recorded in the shared history), completed ones stay as records.

Task management

  • Today view with priority bands, progress bar and a time-remaining estimate; Upcoming and Someday lanes; tags with a checkbox picker; per-person filters; undo everywhere.
  • Real-life recurrence: daily/weekly/monthly plus fortnightly ("every second Thursday"), month-multiples ("quarterly"), and sub-daily ("every three hours" — ticking respawns the next slot, so the tick history is the feed log).
  • Break it down: a daunting task becomes 3–7 tiny first steps (AI, with task-aware fallbacks).
  • Energy matching, easy-wins filter, Just One Thing full-screen focus mode, and a no-shame carry-over: nothing ever goes red; after five quiet days the app gently asks "still matters?"
  • Delegation by voice ("Josh to feed the dog"), assignees, per-task move between spaces.

Spaces & the trust layer

  • Multiple spaces per account — Home + Mum's care + co-parenting — with one Today.
  • Attribution on everything: who added, who ticked, when.
  • Append-only history: the shared record cannot be edited or deleted — a delete leaves a tombstone. Exportable as CSV and print/PDF, always free.
  • Notes never silently diverge: generous share caps with an honest notice on the rare hit.
  • Spaces can close with dignity: Archive keeps the record read-only and out of daily view; "Export & remove" downloads the record then purges it from the device — the foster-carer / placement-end obligation, behind a typed confirmation.
  • Invites are short codes with no secrets inside; anonymous-first accounts with optional Google/email sign-in that links (not replaces) the identity.

Cohort packs — one engine, four audiences

Audiences are served by feature bundles keyed to the coordination load they carry, switched by space type and self-selected cohort — not by forks.

  • AI tone check offers neutral rewrites before words land
  • Ledger with receipts, configurable split (70/30 is real life)
  • Custody days incl. school terms, holidays, and third caregivers ("kids with Grandma")
Caring for someone

The care team

  • Meds & routines: every tick a record; misses flagged; one task per dose
  • PRN interval line: last dose + when the prescriber's gap ends
  • Care journal (voice/typed/photo) + shift handoff
  • Doctor briefing with numeric readings trends (BGL, sats, feeds…)

Reports — the read-back

One overlay, one print path, a different question per audience: the doctor briefing ("what does the GP need to know?"), My week in review ("did this actually help me?"), the family Week report ("who carried what?"), and the co-parenting Records summary ("what would we both sign off on?"). All compose locally in an instant (AI optionally enriches), all itemize the record rather than summarising it away, and all print. The Journal tab is the home of every report, every raw note, and every space's records and ledger.

Onboarding — both doors

  • First run: "What brings you here?" cohort self-selection → a toolkit explainer mapped to the selection → one-tap creation of the right space type → a guided setup checklist → a first-capture coach.
  • Joining by invite (new in v95): a 30-second welcome shaped by the space being joined — what this space does in three lines, the name attribution runs on, and straight into a first capture. This is the foundation for professionally-guided onboarding: a caseworker or OT sets up with a client, and the app carries the welcome from there.

Platform

  • Installable PWA — offline-capable end to end (capture → tasks → reports run with zero network), no app store required. Store distribution in progress (Google Play via TWA; Apple via wrapper).
  • Managed zero-config backend (Firebase, operator-run) with membership-enforcing security rules; a self-hosted option for technical users; on-device AI options for the privacy-sensitive.
  • Consent-first anonymised analytics; first-party operator dashboard; accessibility built in (screen-reader labels, reduced motion, large targets, live-region transcription).
  • Self-diagnosing: Settings → Developer functions ships a "Test AI" health check that walks the AI chain and names the failing step; every fallback is recorded with an honest reason.
  • Store-readiness shipped: privacy policy, terms and support pages, and full in-app account deletion (Apple 5.1.1(v) / Play compliant).

Validation — the 44-persona study

Before this document's last major revision, 44 personas — spanning ADHD students and FIFO workers, six-kid blended families, tri-parenting households, palliative rosters, young carers, NDIS circles, foster carers and more — were built as executable simulations and driven through the real product end-to-end. The study produced 30 ranked findings; the top fifteen (every P0, P1 and P2 item) have since been shipped and re-verified against the personas that surfaced them. The remaining items are growth-tier bets, tracked openly.

The business model, end to end

Who pays, and why they would

The audiences are chosen because their pain is concentrated — they need coordination, not a nicer list — and willingness to pay is proven in-market (the incumbent co-parenting record app charges over $100/year per parent). The buyer is the household organiser, the co-parent who needs a record, or the care team's anchor person.

Pricing principles (constraints on every lever)

  • Never paywall the trust layer. The append-only record, attribution, exports, and leaving-with-your-data are free forever. That's the ethical spine.
  • The app works without paying. Heuristic engine, browser speech, core tasks and spaces stay free — premium buys capacity and convenience, never basic function.
  • Meter what costs money; gift what doesn't. Levers attach to real marginal cost (AI calls, cloud transcription minutes, storage) or clearly-premium surfaces (deep report history) — not arbitrary fences.
  • Members and invites are never gated. Spaces are the growth loop.

Enforcement is already built: per-user daily quotas at the AI proxy (server-side, can't be bypassed client-side), per-capture voice caps, and an entitlement-flag path ready for a payment webhook. Anchor: cheaper than one coffee a month.

Unit economics

Infrastructure runs ≈ 7¢ per family per month at the free tier (managed Firebase + metered AI through one operator key), so roughly 1% paid conversion covers all infrastructure. There is no per-seat marginal cost worth metering: members and invites stay free because each space recruits its own members.

Distribution

  • The space is the growth loop: every user pulls in the 2–6 people they coordinate with; the v95 joiner welcome converts those arrivals instead of dropping them into a bare app.
  • Community channels, persona-mapped: ADHD creators and forums, single-parent and co-parenting groups, carer organisations, school newsletters, FIFO and Defence family networks.
  • Professional referral: OTs, ADHD coaches, family lawyers and settlement caseworkers already teach coordination habits — the guided first-run plus joiner welcome makes the app hand-overable. (A fuller professional mode is a planned bet.)
  • Store presence (in progress) unlocks search intent: "co-parenting record app", "medication tracker for carers", "ADHD todo voice".

Honest limitations

English-only extraction today (a four-tier multilingual plan exists, unscheduled); minute-exact alarms need the store wrapper (the app promises adherence records, not alarms); AI features run on a heuristic fallback wherever AI is off — by design, everything works offline; and the product is in active development with the rough edges that implies.
